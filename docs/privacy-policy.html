Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 7, 2026

foodFinder ("we", "our", or "the app") is a mobile application that helps you discover restaurants nearby. This policy explains what information we collect and how we use it.

1. Information We Collect

Location Data

With your permission, we access your device's GPS location to show restaurants near you. You can also manually set a location using search or the map picker. We do not store your location on any server.

Usage Analytics

We use PostHog, a third-party analytics service, to collect anonymous usage data such as:

  • Which screens you visit
  • Actions you take (e.g., swiping, filtering)
  • App version and device type

This data is anonymized and used solely to improve the app experience. PostHog's privacy policy is available at posthog.com/privacy.

Local Storage

Your liked and dismissed restaurants are stored locally on your device using AsyncStorage. This data never leaves your device.

2. Information We Do Not Collect

  • We do not collect your name, email, or any personal identifiers
  • We do not require account creation or login
  • We do not collect payment information
  • We do not share or sell any data to third parties

3. Third-Party Services

The app uses the following third-party services:

  • Google Places API — to fetch restaurant information. Subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • PostHog — for anonymous usage analytics. Subject to PostHog's Privacy Policy.

4. Data Retention

Location data is used in real time and is not persisted. Analytics data is retained by PostHog according to their data retention policies. Local app data is stored on your device until you clear the app data or uninstall the app.

5. Children's Privacy

foodFinder is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date.

7. Contact Us

If you have questions about this privacy policy, please open an issue on our GitHub repository.
